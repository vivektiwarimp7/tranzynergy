Understand your taxes

Let’s be honest, taxes are complicated. They’re confusing and can be messy. Leave it to professionals to streamline the process for you so you don’t have to stress come tax time. Without considering deductions, expenses or personal income tax brackets, let’s break down the difference between a Sole Proprietor and S-Corporation.

Government tax laws give corporations a tax advantage. Why not take advantage of those laws? That’s where we come in. tranZynergy was created to help freelancers transition to S-Corporations and take advantage of their business potential. There’s no cost to the platform, just a net gain for you. Consider the following example:

Sole Proprietor

$40,000 of 1099 annual income
↓
All income subject to self-employment tax at 15% Pay $6,000 in self-employment tax (40,000x0.15)
↓
$34,000 net income subject to personal income tax bracket If in the 12% tax bracket
↓
$29,920 net income after federal taxes

Corporation (S-Corp)

$40,000 of corporate revenue

↓

$20,000 of W2 salary $20,000 of company profit distribution to owner

↓

All salary subject to FICA tax at 7.5% profit distribution not subject to FICA taxes Pay $1,500 in FICA taxes (20Kx0.075) Company Pays $1,500 in FICA taxes (20Kx0.075)

↓

$18,500 net salary income + $18,500 in profit income as the owner of an S corp

↓

$37,000 in net income subject to personal income tax bracket If in the 12% tax bracket

↓

$32,560 net income after federal taxes



So either pay $6,000 out of pocket to the government or pay $1,500 to the government and pocket the savings. The tranZynergy platform facilitates running your service as a business.
